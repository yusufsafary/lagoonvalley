🌴 The Game

A valley by the sea

Lagoon Valley is a mobile-first 3D farming game built for people who want something calm to tend to. The tide rolls in, changes what you can reach, and rolls back out. Days pass. You settle in.

The World

Where the hills meet the sea

The valley sits in a pocket of land cradled between rolling hills and a coastal lagoon. On one side, farmland stretches toward the morning light. On the other, the water breathes with the tide, swelling and retreating in a rhythm that every farmer here learns to read.

You arrive with a small plot, a handful of seeds, and enough gold to get started. What you do with it is up to you.

The tide is not a backdrop

Most of the coastal farmland floods at high tide. The best plots sit closest to the water, which means they are also the most vulnerable. Knowing when to plant and when to step back is part of the game, not a tutorial to skip past.

Built for Mobile

Hold it in your hands, not on a desk

Lagoon Valley was designed from the ground up for phones. The virtual joystick on the bottom left moves your character. The action button on the bottom right does everything else: till, plant, water, harvest, talk. The inventory bar sits at the bottom where your thumb can reach it.

It works in a browser, which means no install. Tap the link, wait a few seconds for the world to load, and you are in.

Under the Hood

How it is put together

The 3D world runs on Three.js, rendered directly to a canvas element that fills the screen. The build pipeline uses Vite, and the whole thing ships as static files hosted on GitHub Pages.

No backend, no accounts, no tracking. The game state lives in memory while you play. It is a quiet project built because it was interesting to build.
